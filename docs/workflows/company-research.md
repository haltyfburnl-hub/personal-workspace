# Company Research Workflow

Use this workflow when researching a company before an interview, partnership discussion, investment review, or business decision.

## 1. Create a workspace

```bash
python3 scripts/create_workspace.py "Example Company" --type company
```

## 2. Collect source material

Place source notes in the workspace `sources/` folder. Useful source categories include:

- Official website and product pages.
- Annual reports, announcements, or regulatory filings.
- Public news and interviews.
- Job descriptions and recruitment posts.
- Competitor information.
- Your own meeting notes.

Do not commit private source files to the public repository.

## 3. Build the company profile

Start with `company-profile.md` and fill in:

- Basic company information.
- Business model.
- Products or services.
- Market position.
- Growth drivers.
- Main risks.
- Questions to verify.

## 4. Separate facts from judgment

A useful research note should make this distinction clear:

- Facts: source-backed information.
- Inferences: your current interpretation.
- Open questions: items that still need verification.
- Actions: what to do next.

## 5. Prepare outputs

Common final outputs include:

- Interview preparation note.
- Opportunity and risk memo.
- Company comparison table.
- Questions for the hiring manager or business owner.
- Short briefing for a colleague or advisor.
