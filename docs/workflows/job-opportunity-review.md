# Job Opportunity Review Workflow

Use this workflow to decide whether a role is worth pursuing and how to prepare for it.

## 1. Capture the role clearly

Record the job title, company, location, reporting line, compensation range if known, and source of the opportunity.

## 2. Check role fit

Review the role against these dimensions:

- Industry fit.
- Functional fit.
- Seniority and decision authority.
- Expected business problem to solve.
- Personal constraints such as location, family schedule, and income needs.
- Long-term upside.

## 3. Identify risk signals

Look for signals such as:

- Vague role ownership.
- Unrealistic growth targets.
- No clear budget or team.
- High turnover in the same position.
- Founder or owner expectations that do not match resources.
- Compensation that relies too heavily on uncertain future outcomes.

## 4. Prepare interview questions

Good questions should test reality, not just show interest:

- What problem must this role solve in the first 90 days?
- What resources are already in place?
- Which targets are fixed, and which can be negotiated?
- Who has final decision rights?
- What happened to the previous person in this role?

## 5. Decide the next action

End each review with one of four decisions:

- Pursue actively.
- Continue only if key questions are answered.
- Keep warm but do not invest much time.
- Decline.
